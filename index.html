About me

Hi, my name is Yousif Kuwa. I'm 15 years old and I live in New Haven, Connecticut. I am a student at ESUMS. This website showcases the work I completed this year and reflects on what I learned during my screenprinting project.

About the Project

Me and my classmates worked on a screenprinting project. During this project I learned how designs can be transferred onto different materials through the screenprinting process. I worked on creating and preparing my design while following instructions carefully. My design focused on confidence and personal growth. I wanted to show that sometimes people need to take risks and push themselves outside of their comfort zone to become more confident.

Throughout the project I learned the importance of patience, planning, and paying attention to details. Every step of the process had to be completed carefully to achieve the final result.

What Was Used to Create the Screenprint Design

To create my design I used Adobe Photoshop. My design featured a plane and a person jumping out of it to represent confidence. The person in the design is me. My classmates helped take photographs by using a green blanket and a desk to create the effect of me falling through the sky. After uploading the image to a MacBook, I edited it and added the word confidence across my arm. We also used Adobe Illustrator to create measurements for the frame.

21st Century Skill

The 21st-century skill I demonstrated the most was creativity. I had to think about how my design would look when it was printed and make decisions about the overall appearance of the final product. This project helped me improve my creativity and problem-solving skills because I had to plan my design carefully and make adjustments throughout the process.
